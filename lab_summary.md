This lab explored an end-to-end RAG retrieval workflow using a trustworthy AI PDF and a podcast transcript. The notebook first chunked both sources with rich metadata, then compared baseline embedding retrieval with two reranking options: GPT-based relevance scoring and Cohere reranking. It also showed how metadata filtering can restrict search to either the PDF or the transcript, which makes retrieval more precise when source type matters. The overall takeaway is that baseline similarity search is the fastest and simplest approach, but metadata-aware retrieval combined with reranking produces more relevant results and is the better choice when quality matters more than latency. For the PDF, the notebook favors recursive, metadata-rich processing; for the podcast transcript, the same pipeline works well, with LLM or Cohere reranking as an optional quality boost.
