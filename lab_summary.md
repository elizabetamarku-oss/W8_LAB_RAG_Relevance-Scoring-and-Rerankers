For this lab, the recommended strategy depends on the content type. For PDF documents, Recursive Character Chunking with `chunk_size=1000` and `chunk_overlap=200` works best because it respects section and paragraph boundaries, which keeps ideas coherent and reduces mid-sentence breaks. For podcast transcripts, Token-Based Chunking with `chunk_size=500` and `chunk_overlap=50` is the better choice because transcripts are less structured and token-aware splitting maps more naturally to LLM context windows and retrieval workflows. Overall, Fixed-Size chunking is the simplest and fastest option, but it breaks context more often; Recursive Character chunking preserves structure better for documents; Token-Based chunking gives the best fit for LLM-oriented text such as transcripts; and Semantic chunking can improve coherence, but it is slower and more computationally expensive, so it is best treated as an advanced option rather than the default.
